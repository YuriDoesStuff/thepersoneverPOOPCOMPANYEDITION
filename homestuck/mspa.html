what even is an mspa?

an mspaint adventure is a story through images on the internet, with each page having a correlated command and response (ala old adventure games)
that's it really!

in all actuality, mspas can be much more than images on a screen. there's many tropes specifically tied to mspas (usually originating from andrew hussie's work, due to the fact hussie founded mspas as a whole), but this page will primarily focus on the technology behind mspas

initially, the first few mspas were exactly what i described before... mock games in a "choose your own adventure" style with images. but with the third mspa hussie put out, the images began to move

hussie used photoshop to animate all panels seen in problem sleuth, for both visual effects (such as mobster kingpin's eyes here)

and for more traditional animation

another thing to note is that, opposed to previous mspas hussie made, problem sleuth reused a lot of art to help speed up panel output. the reuse of assets also gave the mspa a sprite-based look, which further tied mspas to mock games.

not all panels reused art though!

typically, hand drawn panels were made to move the camera for either dramatic or comedic effect. or hell sometimes both

however, with flash being a better software for both animation and reusing assets, hussie decided to go with adobe flash for his next mspaint adventure!
